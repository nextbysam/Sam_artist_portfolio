My Philosophy

I believe in creating—not competing. Competition is for those who accept the status quo. Instead, I aim for the revolutionary: solving humanity's deepest problems, not just playing the game better.

I'm not here to compete with anyone. I'm here to create something that didn't exist before. Something that matters. An artist doesn't compete with other artists—they express what only they can see.

Isolation isn't loneliness; it's where focus becomes sacred. While everyone else is networking, I'm going deep—obsessively deep—into the problems that actually matter.

You break rules. You experiment. You fail—and you do it again, better. Ideas come through clarity, not chaos. And clarity comes from stillness.

I'm 16. I'm still experimenting, still learning. But I'm not waiting for permission to start. The world doesn't need another person competing for the same prizes. It needs people who see a different game entirely.
